Habit of decisiveness

It seems to be the case that those who get on in life have a habit of descisiveness.
Instead of putting their implementation of ideas off until later -- After some thing which is supposed to happen first -- they just start. Because for them time is money, and good fast implementation is possibly even more money, especially when accounting for compounding of momentum.
So I must adopt this approach:
Part of me is tempted to put off the work until tomorrow morning, but since I've been up all night on a bender, its now 8:50 am, and so leaving it until the morning is actually equivalent to just starting now.
I'm also supposed to be setting off on the bike for Laos this morning, so a start will be all that is possible. But that's alright, because after this caffination wears off, I'll probably be too tired to make any real progress with the website anyway.
